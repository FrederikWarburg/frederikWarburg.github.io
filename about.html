Frederik Warburg

I'm Head of AI at Teton.ai. We are building the next generation of healthcare technology, using computer vision and deep learning to help nurses and patients in hospitals and nursing homes. We work at the forefront of research in 3D reconstruction, human mesh recovery, action recognition, and vision-language models. Please reach out if you are interested in collaborations, research internships, or job opportunities.

Previously, I was a PhD student at the Technical University of Denmark, working on 3D reconstruction, metric learning, and uncertainty quantification. I was supervised by Søren Hauberg (DTU), Javier Civera (University of Zaragoza), and Serge Belongie (Pioneer Centre for AI). I was also a visiting researcher at UC Berkeley, where I worked with Angjoo Kanazawa. During my PhD, I received the Elite Research Travel Grant (2022), which is awarded to 20 talented PhD students in Denmark. My thesis was recognized with the Best Nordic Thesis Award (2025) and DTU's Best PhD Thesis Award (2024). I also interned at Meta in Zurich (2021), Slamcore in London (2020), and Mapillary (2019).

I graduated with honours from the MSc in Mathematical Modelling and Computing. During my studies, I visited the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley in 2020, Marc Pollefeys' Visual Computing Group at ETH Zurich in 2019, and Javier Civera's Computer Vision Group at the University of Zaragoza in 2018.
